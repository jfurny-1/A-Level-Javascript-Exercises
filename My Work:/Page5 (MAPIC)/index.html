Machu Piccu

Click Me Machu Picchu was named one of the New Seven Wonders of the World in 2007. The citadel is located at an impressive altitude of 7,000 feet within the Andes Mountains. Approximately 75% of Machu Picchu's structures are original, making it one of the best-preserved archaeological sites. The Incas built Machu Picchu without using mortar, showcasing their advanced stone-cutting techniques. Machu Picchu’s agricultural sector was designed to cultivate a variety of crops, including maize, potatoes, and quinoa.
