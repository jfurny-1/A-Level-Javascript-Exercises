The Great Pyramids of Giza

Click Me The Great Pyramid of Giza is a defining symbol of Egypt and the last of the ancient Seven Wonders of the World. It was built during the reign of King Khufu (also known as Cheops) of the 4th Dynasty. The pyramid served as the tomb of pharaoh Khufu. It is the largest Egyptian pyramid and the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The corners of the pyramids point to the Temple of Ra at Heliopolis, in concert with the constellation Orion.
